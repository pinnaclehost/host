Cookie Policy

1. What Are Cookies?

By accessing the Site or using any of our online services, you acknowledge and accept that we may place one or more cookies—small text files with unique identification codes—on your device. These may include session cookies, which are deleted when you close your browser, and persistent cookies, which remain on your device until they expire or are manually removed. For guidance on how to manage or disable cookies, please refer to your browser’s “Help” section. Keep in mind that blocking or deleting cookies may limit your access to certain features of Cara Host products and services. We may also utilize cookies from third-party providers (e.g., Google Analytics—refer to https://www.google.com/policies/privacy/partners for more information).

By agreeing to the use of cookies, you grant us permission to automatically collect technical data from your device through methods such as “web beacons” or “clear GIFs” (collectively referred to as “cookies and similar technologies”). This data may include your IP address, device identifiers, browser and operating system information, details of pages viewed before and after visiting our Site, content you engage with, and timestamps of your interactions. We may also monitor how you interact with our emails (e.g., opens, clicks, forwards).

2. Cookies Used by Cara Host

We employ different categories of cookies to deliver and improve our products and services:

Strictly Necessary Cookies: Essential for providing the requested services, including secure communication and protection against attacks. Blocking these cookies will prevent the use of our services.

Preference (Functionality) Cookies: Retain your selections (e.g., language, region, login credentials) and balance server load to enhance performance and response time.

Statistics (Performance) Cookies: Collect anonymous data about your usage (e.g., pages visited, links clicked) to help improve your user experience.

Marketing Cookies: Monitor your online behavior to show relevant advertisements and limit repeated exposure to the same ads.

4. Cookie Duration

Session cookies are removed when you close your browser, while persistent cookies remain until they expire (usually between 30 minutes and two years) or are manually deleted. You can manage or disable cookies through your browser settings (generally found under “Tools” or “Preferences”). Disabling cookies may affect certain functionalities of the site. For more detailed instructions, consult your browser’s help resources or visit https://www.allaboutcookies.org.

5. “Do Not Track” Signals

As there is no uniform industry standard for handling “Do Not Track” signals, we do not modify our data collection practices in response to such signals.
